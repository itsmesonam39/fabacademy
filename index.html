Core Technologies

The three pillars that make the Check-IT Console work.

01. RFID Automation

Contactless Identification

The Tech: Replacing manual barcodes with NFC/RFID tags (MFRC522) for instant "Tap-and-Go" user and item authentication.

02. IoT Connectivity

Cloud Integration

The Tech: Using the ESP32 microcontroller to bridge physical hardware with the web, sending data directly to Google Sheets via REST API.

03. System Integration

Unified Architecture

The Tech: Combining SPI displays and RFID protocols into a single, compact 3.3V logic circuit without signal conflicts.

THE CHECK-IT CONSOLE

A Solid-State Inventory Gateway.

1. Defining the Problem

The Pain Point: Currently, Our school faces issues with managing the laptops.

The Solution: The Check-IT Console digitizes the checkout process, creating a searchable digital log in the cloud with better management.

2. Mechanical Design (Fusion 360)

To make the design process easier to follow, I have grouped the 40 steps into 4 key phases. Click a phase to expand it.

PHASE 1: THE MAIN BODY & ENCLOSURE (Steps 1-12)

Step 1

I started by creating a new sketch on the top plane using the Rectangle tool (14cm x 11cm) to define the footprint.

Step 2

I extruded the rectangle upwards by 7.00 cm to create the main solid block body.

Step 3

To create the angled face, I sketched a large triangle on the side face, setting the bottom point 2.50 cm up from the edge.

Step 4

I extruded the triangle with a "Cut" operation to slice off the top section, creating the console's slope.

Step 5

I used the Fillet tool on the vertical corners (1.00 cm radius) to smooth the device edges.

Step 6

I used the Shell tool with a thickness of 0.30 cm to hollow out the block into an enclosure.

Step 7

I sketched a new rectangle (14cm x 11.885cm) to create the lid that matches the slope length.

Step 8

I extruded the lid sketch by 0.30 cm as a "New Body" so it remains separate from the box.

Step 9

Using the Move/Copy tool, I rotated and positioned the lid to sit flush on the angled opening.

Step 10

I filleted the lid corners (1.00 cm) to match the curvature of the main body below it.

Step 11

I applied a smaller 0.3 cm fillet to the vertical edges of the case for a softer finish.

Step 12

I used the Measure tool to verify internal dimensions, ensuring components would fit inside the 12cm width.

PHASE 2: COMPONENT MOCKUPS (Steps 13-22)

Step 13

I sketched the Screen component with four mounting holes using construction lines for centering.

Step 14

I extruded the screen body (0.5cm thick) as a New Body representing the LCD hardware.

Step 15

I cut the four corner holes through the screen body to allow for mounting screws.

Step 16

I sketched the RFID Reader (6cm x 4cm) with 0.3cm mounting holes.

Step 17

I extruded the RFID sketch (0.3cm thick) as a New Body.

Step 18

I cut the mounting holes through the RFID body.

Step 19

I sketched the ESP32 microcontroller (5.2cm x 2.8cm) with 0.28cm corner holes.

Step 20

I extruded the ESP32 body to 0.5cm thickness.

Step 21

I cut the mounting holes through the ESP32 body.

Step 22

Using Move/Copy, I positioned the ESP32 board inside the bottom of the enclosure.

PHASE 3: PORTS & MOUNTING (Steps 23-30)

Step 23

I sketched circles on the board surface to define the diameter of the support standoffs.

Step 24

I sketched a 1.00cm x 0.45cm rectangle on the side wall for the USB power port.

Step 25

I extruded the USB rectangle with a "Cut" operation to create the port opening.

Step 26

I filleted the USB port edges (0.1cm) for a cleaner look.

Step 27

I extruded the standoff sketches downwards using "To Object" (selecting the floor) to create the pillars.

Step 28

I sketched a 5.00cm x 3.80cm rectangle on the lid for the screen window.

Step 29

I cut the screen window through the lid using Extrude.

Step 30

I moved the screen component into place, fitting it snugly into the lid cutout.

PHASE 4: DETAILING & ASSEMBLY (Steps 31-40)

Step 31

I sketched support circles (0.08cm) on the back of the screen component.

Step 32

I extruded the screen supports "To Object" to connect them to the main body.

Step 33

I arranged the RFID and ESP32 components inside to ensure no collisions occurred.

Step 34

I sketched concentric circles on the top face for the button layout.

Step 35

I extruded the button rings downwards (-0.1cm) to create tactile grooves.

Step 36

I filleted the outer edges of the button rings (0.1cm).

Step 37

I filleted the inner edges of the button grooves (0.1cm) for a soft feel.

Step 38

I used a section view to verify internal heights and clearances.

Step 39

I sketched four 0.50cm circles on the inner lid face for screw pillars.

Step 40

I extruded the lid pillars "To Object" to connect firmly with the main body.
